Emergency Water Storage Methods

Learn about effective methods for storing water in emergency situations.

Store in a Cool, Dark Place

To prevent bacterial growth and chemical leaching, store your water containers in a cool, dark place. Direct sunlight and high temperatures can promote the growth of algae and bacteria and may cause harmful chemicals to leach from plastic containers. Ideal storage locations include basements, closets, or under sinks.

Seal Containers Properly

Properly sealing water containers is crucial to avoid contamination. Use containers with tight-fitting lids, preferably with rubber gaskets. If your containers do not have secure lids, you can use plastic wrap under the lid or additional sealing methods to ensure no contaminants enter the water.

Consider Purification and Treatment

If there is any doubt about the quality of your stored water, it is essential to treat it before consumption. Common methods include:

  • Boiling: Boil water for at least one minute to kill pathogens.
  • Filtration: Use a water filter that removes bacteria, protozoa, and viruses.
  • Chemical Disinfection: Add water purification tablets or drops, following the manufacturer's instructions.

Label and Date Containers

To keep track of your stored water, label each container with the date it was filled and any treatment methods used. This practice helps ensure you use the oldest water first and monitor how long your water has been stored.

Emergency Water Sources

In addition to storing water, identify alternative emergency water sources. These can include:

  • Natural Sources: Rivers, streams, and lakes (ensure you purify the water).
  • Home Sources: Water heaters, ice cubes, and toilet tanks (non-chemical treated water).
  • Portable Filtration Devices: Personal water filters, portable filtration systems, and purification straws.

Emergency Planning

Integrate water storage into your overall emergency preparedness plan. Alongside water, ensure you have:

  • Food Supplies: Non-perishable food items that can last for several days.
  • Medical Kits: First aid supplies, prescription medications, and other essential medical items.
  • Communication Plans: Ways to stay in touch with family members and emergency services, including having a list of emergency contacts and a battery-operated or hand-crank radio.
